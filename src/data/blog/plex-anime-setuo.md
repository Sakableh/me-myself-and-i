Today I finally did something I’ve been meaning to fix forever: my anime setup in Plex.

For the longest time, Plex behaved like a chaotic gremlin — randomly choosing English dub, picking the wrong subtitles, or pretending an entire season didn’t exist. Every time I hit play, I felt like I was negotiating with the app.

So I cleaned it up. And honestly? It feels good.

* * *

## Step 1: Finding Better Releases

I started using [**releases.moe**](<https://releases.moe/?utm_source=chatgpt.com>), and wow — why didn’t I do this sooner?

Instead of gambling on random versions with questionable subs, I could actually compare release groups, find consistent batches, and choose something that wouldn’t break my library. It felt like upgrading from chaos to curated order.

* * *

## Step 2: Teaching Plex to Respect My Preferences

The real magic happened when I discovered Plex lets you set **language preferences per series**.

So for anime, I told Plex:

  * Audio → **Japanese**
  * Subs → **English**

Simple. Clean. Beautiful.

Now every episode starts correctly — no fumbling through menus like I'm defusing a bomb.

* * *

## Final Thought

It sounds silly, but fixing this made my library finally feel like _mine_. No more fighting Plex. No more re-selecting subtitles every episode. Just: play → enjoy → next episode.

Small win… but a satisfying one.

And future me will thank present me every time I hit "Continue Watching." 🍿✨
